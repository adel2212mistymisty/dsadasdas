Meet the legends of the club, those who have built and carried the club to what is it today. Although every player ever in our club has been excellent, these players have shown exceptional talent throughout their reigning years.

Sir Kenny Dalglish


1977 - 1985


Often reffered to as 'King Kenny' by fans, Dalglish came over from Celtic during the late 1970s, Dalglish spent his 18 years as a player for the reds and left the playing field. as a red in 1985. He was then the manager from 1985-1991 where he left Liverpool due to the Hilsborough Disaster. Over his career, he achieved the Ballon D’or silver award in 1983, PFA Players’ of the Year in 1983 as well as the FWA Footballer of the Year award in 1979 and 1983. He also racked up a total of 172 Goals in 515 appearances.

Phil Neal


1974 - 1985


Phil Neal is one of Liverpool's most decorated players. A player selected to succeed Graeme Souness as a captain, Phil Neal’s loyalty and commitment was shown strikingly. He has contributed to 4 League titles, 4 European Cups and 1 FA Cup. Phil Neal had shown exceptional consistency and dedication towards the club, both for domestic games and games outside of England.

Steven Gerrard


1998 - 2015


Steven Gerrard is arguably the best player Liverpool has ever had. A leader on and off the pitch. The loyalty and attitude that Gerrard has had for the club was exceptional throughout his career, inspiring teammates, leading by example and his ability of making quick but intelligent decisions on the pitch. Being one of the longest serving players of Liverpool and a born and bred scouser, Gerrard has played the majority of his career here at Liverpool. Despite not having as many trophies as others, he remains one of the most influential midfielders of all time. Over his time in Liverpool, he has won one UEFA Champions League, 2 FA Cups and 3 League Cups

Graeme Souness


1978 - 1984


During the club’s dominant era during the 1980s, Souness had played a crucial role in securing the 5 League titles, 3 European Cups, and 4 League Cups. He was a fierce but strong tactician and leader of the team, leading by example. Not only was he crucial domestically, but he played a key role contributing to tournaments and games continent-wide. His pivotal and commanding role will forever be recognised and cemented in history.

Fernando Torres


2007 - 2011


Fernando Torres, who is often known as “El Niño” will forever remain one of Liverpool's most iconic and beloved attackers to ever grace the club. Joining in 2007 from Atlético Madrid. Known for his incredible pace, skill and finishing, El Niño for his time at Liverpool was well known as a prolific scorer. Although he only played for 4 years, he managed to score 81 goals in 142 appearances. His charisma and talent engraved himself into the heart of thousands and thousands of Liverpool fans.

Alan Hansen


1977 - 1991


Coming over in 1977, Alan Hansen was a key defensive figure during Liverpool’s 1980 era. Well known for his composure on the ball and tactical awareness, he soon established himself as a strong and established centre-back. Over his 13 years in Merseyside, Alan Hansen has won 8 League titles, 3 European Cups and 2 FA Cups in which he formed stylish defensive partnerships with his centre-back duo.

Ray Clemence


1967 - 1981


Ray Clemence is definitely one of Liverpool’s most impactful and greatest goalkeepers. Playing a fundamental role during the 1970s and 1980s. Joining in 1967, over his time at Liverpool, he has won 5 League titles, 3 European Cups, 2 FA Cups and 1 UEFA Cup. Being a reliable and consistent figure between the posts, his contributions were integral to Liverpool's dominance and he now remains as one of the best goalkeepers Liverpool have ever had.

Ian Rush


1980-1986 & 1987 - 1996


Ian Rush, arguably Liverpool's most lethal and iconic strikers. Known for his destructive shooting capabilities. Being Liverpool’s all-time top scorer having 346 goals in 660 appearances, he was loved at Anfield, but feared everywhere else. His remarkable shooting skills, paired with his great sense of positioning named him a threat for many oppositions. Over at Liverpool, Ian Rush had won 5 League titles, 2 European Cups, and 2 FA Cups.

John Barnes


1987 - 1997


At Liverpool, John Barnes was a dazzling and stylish winger. Complete will skill, creativity and speed, John Barnes was one of the players who dominated the left hand side of the pitch. His capability to glide swiftly past defenders like a wave quickly struck the attention of fans. His performances were key to Liverpool's attack during the 1980s and 1990s. He won 2 League Titles, 2 FA Cups, and 1 League Cup.
